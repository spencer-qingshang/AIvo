# Modern Family S01E01 语法分析

## Scene 1: Dunphy Household

1.  **Kids! Breakfast!**
    *   **Analysis**: Exclamatory noun phrases. "Kids" is a vocative (calling someone). "Breakfast" indicates the reason for calling.
    *   **Translation**: 孩子们！吃早饭了！

2.  **Phil, would you get them?**
    *   **Analysis**: Interrogative sentence (Question).
        *   *Phil*: Vocative (Proper Noun).
        *   *would*: Auxiliary verb (used for polite requests).
        *   *you*: Subject (Pronoun).
        *   *get*: Main verb (Transitive).
        *   *them*: Direct object (Pronoun).
    *   **Translation**: 菲尔，你能去叫他们吗？

3.  **Yeah, just a sec.**
    *   **Analysis**: Elliptical sentence (Fragment).
        *   *Yeah*: Affirmative interjection.
        *   *just a sec*: Idiomatic phrase (short for "just a second").
    *   **Translation**: 好，等一下。

4.  **Kids!**
    *   **Analysis**: Vocative noun.
    *   **Translation**: 孩子们！

5.  **That is totaled.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative sentence.
        *   *That*: Subject (Demonstrative Pronoun).
        *   *is*: Linking verb (Be).
        *   *totaled*: Adjective (Participle), meaning "completely wrecked/destroyed".
    *   **Translation**: 那东西彻底报废了。

6.  **Okay.**
    *   **Analysis**: Interjection.
    *   **Translation**: 好吧。

7.  **Kids, get down here!**
    *   **Analysis**: Imperative sentence (Command).
        *   *Kids*: Vocative.
        *   *get*: Verb (Imperative).
        *   *down here*: Adverbial phrase (Direction/Location).
    *   **Translation**: 孩子们，下来！

8.  **Why are you guys yelling at us when we're way upstairs?**
    *   **Analysis**: Complex Interrogative sentence.
        *   *Why*: Interrogative adverb.
        *   *are*: Auxiliary verb.
        *   *you guys*: Subject (Informal plural you).
        *   *yelling*: Main verb (Present Continuous).
        *   *at us*: Prepositional phrase (Target).
        *   *when we're way upstairs*: Adverbial clause of time/condition. *way* emphasizes "upstairs".
    *   **Translation**: 我们在楼上那么远，你们干嘛还要吼我们？

9.  **Just text me.**
    *   **Analysis**: Imperative sentence.
        *   *Just*: Adverb (Only/Simply).
        *   *text*: Verb.
        *   *me*: Indirect object (or Direct object depending on analysis).
    *   **Translation**: 发短信给我就行了。

10. **All right, that's not gonna happen.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative sentence.
        *   *All right*: Interjection.
        *   *that*: Subject.
        *   *is not gonna (going to) happen*: Verb phrase (Future tense with "going to").
    *   **Translation**: 好吧，那是不可能的。

11. **And, wow, you're not wearing that outfit.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative sentence (Negative).
        *   *you*: Subject.
        *   *are not wearing*: Verb phrase (Present Continuous).
        *   *that outfit*: Direct object.
    *   **Translation**: 哇，你不能穿那套衣服。

12. **What's wrong with it?**
    *   **Analysis**: Interrogative sentence.
        *   *What*: Subject (Interrogative Pronoun).
        *   *is*: Verb.
        *   *wrong*: Adjective (Subject complement).
        *   *with it*: Prepositional phrase.
    *   **Translation**: 这衣服怎么了？

13. **Honey, do you have anything to say to your daughter about her skirt?**
    *   **Analysis**: Interrogative sentence.
        *   *Honey*: Vocative.
        *   *do*: Auxiliary.
        *   *you*: Subject.
        *   *have*: Verb.
        *   *anything*: Direct object.
        *   *to say...*: Infinitive phrase modifying "anything".
    *   **Translation**: 亲爱的，关于你女儿的裙子你有什么要说的吗？

14. **Sorry. Yeah, that looks really cute, sweetheart.**
    *   **Analysis**:
        *   *Sorry*: Apology.
        *   *that*: Subject.
        *   *looks*: Linking verb (Sensory).
        *   *really cute*: Adjective phrase (Subject complement).
        *   *sweetheart*: Vocative.
    *   **Translation**: 抱歉。那个，看起来很可爱，宝贝。

15. **No, it's way too short.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative.
        *   *it*: Subject (referring to skirt).
        *   *is*: Verb.
        *   *way too short*: Adjective phrase (*way* intensifies *too*).
    *   **Translation**: 不，太短了。

16. **People know you're a girl. You don't need to prove it to them.**
    *   **Analysis**: Two Declarative sentences.
        *   1: *People* (S) *know* (V) *[that] you're a girl* (Noun Clause - Object).
        *   2: *You* (S) *don't need* (V) *to prove it to them* (Infinitive phrase - Object).
    *   **Translation**:大家都知道你是女孩。你不需要向他们证明这一点。

17. **Luke got his head stuck in the banister again.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative.
        *   *Luke*: Subject.
        *   *got*: Verb (Causative/Resultative usage).
        *   *his head*: Object.
        *   *stuck*: Past Participle (Object complement).
        *   *in the banister*: Prepositional phrase.
        *   *again*: Adverb.
    *   **Translation**: Luke的头又卡在栏杆里了。

18. **I got it. Where's the baby oil?**
    *   **Analysis**:
        *   1: *I got it*: Idiomatic (I will handle it / I understand). *I* (S), *got* (V), *it* (O).
        *   2: *Where*: Interrogative adverb. *is*: Verb. *the baby oil*: Subject.
    *   **Translation**: 我来处理。婴儿油在哪？

19. **It's in our bedside… I don't know. Find it.**
    *   **Analysis**: Fragmented dialogue.
        *   *It's in our bedside...*: Prepositional phrase indicating location.
        *   *Find it*: Imperative.
    *   **Translation**: 在我们要床头……我不知道。自己找。

20. **I was out of control growing up.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative.
        *   *I*: Subject.
        *   *was*: Verb.
        *   *out of control*: Adjective phrase (Subject complement).
        *   *growing up*: Participle phrase (Time/Manner).
    *   **Translation**: 我长大的时候很叛逆（失控）。

21. **I just don't want my kids to make the same bad mistakes I made.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative.
        *   *I*: Subject.
        *   *don't want*: Verb.
        *   *my kids*: Object.
        *   *to make...*: Infinitive phrase (Object complement).
        *   *mistakes [that] I made*: Relative clause modifying "mistakes".
    *   **Translation**: 我只是不想让我的孩子犯我犯过的那些错误。

22. **If Haley never wakes up on a beach in Florida half-naked, I've done my job.**
    *   **Analysis**: Conditional sentence (Type 1/Mixed).
        *   *If clause*: *Haley* (S) *never wakes up* (V) *on a beach...* (Loc) *half-naked* (Adj - manner/state).
        *   *Main clause*: *I* (S) *'ve done* (V - Present Perfect) *my job* (O).
    *   **Translation**: 如果Haley永远不会半裸着在佛罗里达的海滩上醒来，那我的工作就做到了。

## Scene 2: Pritchett Household (Soccer Field)

23. **Vamos, Manny, kick it! Kick it! Don't let him kick it!**
    *   **Analysis**: Imperatives / Exclamations.
        *   *Vamos*: Spanish for "Let's go".
        *   *kick*: Verb. *it*: Object.
        *   *Don't let*: Negative Imperative. *him*: Object. *kick it*: Infinitive phrase (Object complement without 'to').
    *   **Translation**: 上啊，Manny，踢！踢！别让他踢！

24. **He tripped him, Jay. Where's the penalty?**
    *   **Analysis**:
        *   1: *He* (S) *tripped* (V) *him* (O).
        *   2: *Where*: Interrogative. *is*: Verb. *the penalty*: Subject.
    *   **Translation**: 他绊倒了他，Jay。罚球在哪？

25. **Gloria, they're 0 and 6. Let's take it down a notch.**
    *   **Analysis**:
        *   1: *they*: Subject. *are*: Verb. *0 and 6*: Subject complement (Score/Record).
        *   2: *Let's*: Imperative (Let us). *take it down a notch*: Idiom (Relax/Calm down).
    *   **Translation**: Gloria，他们已经0比6输了。咱们消消气（别那么激动）。

26. **Jay's from the city, he has a big business.**
    *   **Analysis**: Compound Declarative.
        *   1: *Jay* (S) *is* (V) *from the city* (Prep Phrase).
        *   2: *he* (S) *has* (V) *a big business* (O).
    *   **Translation**: Jay来自城里，他有很大的生意。

27. **I come from a small village, very poor, but very, very beautiful.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative.
        *   *I*: Subject. *come*: Verb. *from a small village*: Prep phrase.
        *   *very poor, but...*: Adjective phrases modifying "village" (Appositive).
    *   **Translation**: 我来自一个小村庄，很穷，但非常非常美丽。

28. **It is the number one village in all Colombia for all the… What's the word? Murders.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative.
        *   *It*: Subject. *is*: Verb. *the number one village*: Subject complement.
        *   *Murders*: Noun (The missing word).
    *   **Translation**: 那是整个哥伦比亚数一数二的村庄，因为那里有最多的……那个词叫什么？谋杀案。

29. **Come on, Coach, you gotta take that kid out.**
    *   **Analysis**: Imperative / Statement.
        *   *you*: Subject. *gotta* (got to): Semi-modal (have to). *take out*: Phrasal verb (remove/substitute).
    *   **Translation**: 拜托，教练，你得把那孩子换下来。

30. **You wanna take him out? How about I take you out?**
    *   **Analysis**:
        *   1: *You*: Subject. *wanna* (want to): Verb phrase. *take him out*: Object phrase.
        *   2: *How about*: Idiomatic phrase for suggestion/threat. *I take you out*: Clause (here meaning "fight/kill you").
    *   **Translation**: 你想把他弄出去？那我把你“弄出去”怎么样？

31. **Why don't you worry about your son?**
    *   **Analysis**: Interrogative (Suggestion/Criticism).
        *   *Why don't you*: Phrase used for advice/criticism.
        *   *worry*: Verb.
    *   **Translation**: 你干嘛不管管你自己的儿子？

32. **He spent the first half with his hand in his pants.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative.
        *   *He*: Subject. *spent*: Verb. *the first half*: Object (Time).
        *   *with his hand in his pants*: Prepositional phrase (Manner/State).
    *   **Translation**: 他上半场一直把手插在裤子里。

33. **I'm Josh, Ryan's dad.**
    *   **Analysis**: Introduction. *Subject + Verb + Subject Complement (Appositive)*.
    *   **Translation**: 我是Josh，Ryan的爸爸。

34. **Hi, I'm Gloria Pritchett, Manny's mother.**
    *   **Analysis**: Introduction.
    *   **Translation**: 嗨，我是Gloria Pritchett，Manny的妈妈。

35. **And this must be your dad.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative (Inference).
        *   *this*: Subject. *must be*: Verb phrase (Model of deduction). *your dad*: Subject Complement.
    *   **Translation**: 这位肯定是你的爸爸。

36. **No, no, that's funny. Actually, no, I'm her husband.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative (Correction).
        *   *I*: Subject. *'m*: Verb. *her husband*: Subject Complement.
    *   **Translation**: 不不，真幽默。实际上，我是她丈夫。

37. **Don't be fooled by the… Give me a second here.**
    *   **Analysis**:
        *   1: *Don't be fooled*: Passive Imperative.
        *   2: *Give*: Imperative. *me*: Indirect Object. *a second*: Direct Object.
    *   **Translation**: 别被这个……给我一点时间（我想想）。

## Scene 3: Tucker-Pritchett Flight

38. **Who's a good girl? Who's that?**
    *   **Analysis**: Interrogative (Baby talk).
    *   **Translation**: 谁是乖女孩？那是谁呀？

39. **We just adopted her from Vietnam and we're bringing her home for the first time.**
    *   **Analysis**: Compound Sentence.
        *   1: *We* (S) *adopted* (V) *her* (O) *from Vietnam* (Prep).
        *   2: *we* (S) *'re bringing* (V - Present Continuous for future/process) *her* (O) *home* (Adv).
    *   **Translation**: 我们刚从越南领养了她，正第一次带她回家。

40. **She's an angel. You and your wife must be thrilled.**
    *   **Analysis**:
        *   1: *She* is an *angel* (Metaphor).
        *   2: *You and your wife* (S). *must be* (V - Deduction). *thrilled* (Adj).
    *   **Translation**: 她真是个天使。你和你太太肯定高兴坏了。

41. **Daddy needed snacks.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative.
        *   *Daddy*: Subject (Third person reference to self).
        *   *needed*: Verb.
        *   *snacks*: Object.
    *   **Translation**: 爸爸需要点零食。

42. **We have been together for five years now.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative.
        *   *We*: Subject.
        *   *have been*: Verb (Present Perfect).
        *   *together*: Adjective/Adverb.
        *   *for five years*: Time duration.
    *   **Translation**: 我们在一起已经五年了。

43. **And we just decided we really wanted to have a baby.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative.
        *   *we*: Subject. *decided*: Verb.
        *   *we really wanted to have a baby*: Noun clause (Object of decided).
    *   **Translation**: 我们刚刚决定真的很想要个孩子。

44. **Look at that baby with those cream puffs.**
    *   **Analysis**: Imperative.
        *   *Look at*: Phrasal Verb.
        *   *that baby*: Object.
        *   *with those cream puffs*: Prepositional phrase (identifying usage, mistakenly referring to Cam).
    *   **Translation**: 看那个拿着泡芙的孩子（这里被误认为是那个“大个子宝宝”）。

45. **This baby would have grown up in a crowded orphanage if it wasn't for us "cream puffs."**
    *   **Analysis**: Conditional Sentence (Type 3/Mixed).
        *   *Main*: *This baby* (S) *would have grown up* (V) *in a crowded orphanage* (Loc).
        *   *If*: *if it wasn't for* (Idiom: Were it not for) *us "cream puffs"*.
    *   **Translation**: 如果不是因为我们这些“奶油泡芙（娘娘腔）”，这孩子本来会在拥挤的孤儿院长大。

46. **Love knows no race, creed or gender.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative (Proverbial style).
        *   *Love*: Subject.
        *   *knows*: Verb.
        *   *no race, creed or gender*: Compound Object.
    *   **Translation**: 爱是不分种族、信仰或性别的。

47. **Shame on you, you small-minded, ignorant few.**
    *   **Analysis**: Exclamatory / Imperative.
        *   *(Let there be) Shame on you*: Idiom.
        *   *you small-minded, ignorant few*: Appositive/Vocative phrase describing the subjects.
    *   **Translation**: 你们这些心胸狭窄、无知的人，真该感到羞耻。

48. **She's got the cream puffs.**
    *   **Analysis**: Declarative.
        *   *She*: Subject (referring to Lily).
        *   *has got* (has): Verb.
        *   *the cream puffs*: Object.
    *   **Translation**: （原来）是她拿着泡芙。

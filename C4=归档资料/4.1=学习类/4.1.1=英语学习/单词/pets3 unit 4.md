Christine Todd Whitman, the former head of the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, is in the hot seat over her role in the downplaying of the health dangers for New York City residents after 9/11.

美国环保署（EPA）前署长克里斯汀·托德·惠特曼（Christine Todd Whitman）因在 9/11 事件后淡化纽约市居民健康风险的问题而备受指责。

A report by the EPA inspector general says Whitman assured the public that the air was safe before testing was conclusive.

据环保署监察长的一份报告显示，在测试结果尚未得出结论之前，惠特曼就向公众保证空气是安全的。

In addition, all EPA statements were required to be screened by the White House.

此外，所有环保署的声明都需要经过白宫审查。

The long-term effect of breathing in air polluted with chemicals is unknown.

吸入被化学物质污染的空气的长期影响目前尚不明确。

But New York Rep. Jerrold Nadler accuses the administration of covering up a potential health danger in order to get the economy up and running.

但纽约州众议员杰罗尔德·纳德勒（Jerrold Nadler）指责政府为了尽快恢复经济运转而掩盖潜在的健康风险。

"Many people will die early because of this," says Nadler.

纳德勒表示：“许多人会因此早逝。”

Whitman says that she did not object when the White House edited out cautionary notes by EPA scientists.

惠特曼表示，当白宫删减环保署科学家的警示性说明时，她并未提出反对。

She believes that much of the data were open to interpretation, and the White House appropriately reached for the more reassuring analysis.

她认为，许多数据本身就存在解读空间，而白宫选择了更能让人安心的分析结果是合理的。

But New York Sen. Hillary Clinton is calling for an investigation, saying somebody surely leaned on the EPA to lie, which Whitman strongly denies.

然而，纽约州参议员希拉里·克林顿（Hillary Clinton）呼吁对此展开调查，并表示肯定有人施压环保署撒谎，而惠特曼对此坚决否认。
